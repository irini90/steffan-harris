Manual Therapy

Manual Therapy is a hands-on technique used by physiotherapists to treat musculoskeletal pain and dysfunction. It involves a range of physical techniques applied directly to the body to alleviate pain, improve mobility, and enhance overall function. Here's a brief overview of what manual therapy entails.

Gentle, controlled movements applied to joints and soft tissues to improve range of motion and reduce stiffness. This technique aims to restore normal movement patterns without causing pain. High-velocity, low-amplitude thrusts applied to joints to improve mobility and reduce pain. This technique often results in a "pop" sound as gas bubbles in the joint are released.
